St. Martin-pride of Bangladesh

St. Martin's Island is a small island (area only 3 km2) in the northeastern part of the Bay of Bengal, about 9 km south of the tip of the Cox's Bazar-Teknaf peninsula, and forming the southernmost part of Bangladesh. There is a small adjoining island that is separated at high tide, called Chera Dwip.

Sylhet: The Hidden Beauty of Nature

Sylhet is Located on the right bank of the Surma River, Sylhet has a subtropical climate and lush highland terrain.Sylhet is one of the most important spiritual and cultural centres in Bangladesh.The city produces the highest amount of tea and gas in the country.

Sundarban - The Mysterious Mangroves

Sundarbans is a mangrove area in the delta formed by the confluence of the Ganges, Brahmaputra and Meghna Rivers in the Bay of Bengal. It spans from the Hooghly River in India's state of West Bengal to the Baleswar River in Bangladesh's division of Khulna.
